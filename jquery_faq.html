How much land is protected as a National Park?
The National Park Service protects over 84 million acres of wild landscapes and historic sites.
Which National Park is the oldest?
Yellowstone National Park (in Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana) is the oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872
Is there anything big in America's National Parks?
National parks are home to life on a big scale: Sequoia trees are some of the world's largest living things, and Alaskan brown bears are some of the world's largest carnivores.
No... I mean big big.
Seriously, California's Sequoia National Park is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
BIGGER!?!
Ribbon Falls in Yosemite is nine times taller than Niagara Falls; and Death Valley National Park is the largest national park in the lower 48 U.S. states, stretching 5,300 square miles or 3.4 million acres.Is that big enough?
Whats the deal with that geyser?
Old Faithful erupts every 35 to 120 minutes for 1 1/2 to 5 minutes. Its maximum height ranges from 90 to 184 feet. It is not the biggest or the most regular geyser in Yellowstone but it is the biggest regular geyser.
Are there and impressive cave systems in the National Park System?
Carlsbad Caverns National Park in New Mexico is home to the nation's deepest cave, which is 1,593 feet deep; and Mammoth Cave National Park in Kentucky has the longest cave system in the world with more than 3,454 mapped miles.
Are parks all just nature and stuff?
National parks are actually the home to a former federal prison, Alcatraz in Golden Gate National Recreation Area, California, and a leper colony, Molokai Island Hansen's Disease Settlement in Kalaupapa National Historic Site, Hawaii.
Are National Parks and National Monuments the same thing?
National parks and national monuments are sometimes confused. National parks are chosen for their natural beauty, unique geological features, and unusual ecosystems.
Is there a National PArk in every state?
Only one state in the country is not lucky enough to currently have either a national park or national monument. It is actually the country’s first state, Delaware.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • Area: 1,902 mi²
  • Established: February 26, 1919
  • Home of the Grand Canyon...
  • Viewpoints include Mather Point, Yavapai Observation Station and architect Mary Colter’s Lookout Studio and her Desert View Watchtower.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Area: 3,471 mi²
  • Established: March 1, 1872
  • Home of Old Faithful‎
  • The Yellowstone National Park boundaries were drawn in 1872 to include all the geothermal basins in the area.

Sequoia National Park

  • Area: 631 mi²
  • Established: September 25, 1890
  • Home of huge sequoia trees
  • Sequoia National Park is adjacent to Kings Canyon National Park in California's southern Sierra Nevada mountains.
